# Brand & Copywriting Guidelines: The Rogue Puffin
*Last Updated: June 22, 2026*

This document serves as the absolute ground truth for all product positioning, copywriting, and interface design decisions. Any AI assistant or developer working on this codebase must read this document first to maintain consistency and prevent regression to generic speed-reading templates.

---

## 1. Brand Philosophy: Learning Mastery
Traditional speed-reading courses focus on a raw visual trick (skimming) which sacrifices comprehension for speed. We reject this. 

**The Rogue Puffin** is a **Learning Mastery** platform. We teach meta-learning (how to learn). Speed reading is not the destination; it is simply the visual intake mechanism (Pillar 1) that feeds a deeper cognitive synthesis (Pillar 2) and permanent recall (Pillar 3) pipeline.

---

## 2. The Three Pillars: Definitions & Copy Constraints

### Pillar 1: Input (Focus & Ingestion)
* **What it IS:** Neurological visual efficiency. Training eye muscles to move fluidly, defeating visual regression (rereading), overcoming starting friction (the mental energy to begin studying), and decoupling reading from the inner vocalization voice (subvocalization bypass).
* **What it is NOT:** A speed trick, a skimming hack, or just "finishing coursework in half the time."
* **Approved Terminology:** Visual processing efficiency, eye regression control, focus gating, audiomotor decoupling, starting friction.
* **Prohibited Phrasing:** "Visual ingestion calibration" (too complex), "Skim textbook pages" (implies low quality).

### Pillar 2: Process (Active Synthesis)
* **What it IS:** Actively breaking down and structuring information. Extracting the core 5% of value, identifying the author's primary thesis, and mapping concepts into non-linear, structured mind maps.
* **What it is NOT:** Passive reading, highlight-cramming, or summary generation.
* **Approved Terminology:** Active synthesis, conceptual mapping, thesis extraction, mental scaffolding.
* **Prohibited Phrasing:** Plain "summarization" (implies passive compression).

### Pillar 3: Memory (Permanent Recall)
* **What it IS:** Moving information from working memory to long-term storage using scientifically verified cognitive principles.
* **What it is NOT:** Rote memorization, cramming, or re-reading notes.
* **Approved Terminology:** Spaced retrieval practice, active recall, Ebbinghaus forgetting curve, Second Brain indexing.
* **Prohibited Phrasing:** "Cramming tricks."

## 3. Copywriting Guardrails

### The Tone
* **Authentic & Founder-Led:** Grounded in Richard's 30-year mission. Speaks from personal experience ("I was angry that no one taught me this").
* **Academic yet Accessible:** Simple, solid, and trustworthy. We speak the language of cognitive science (for educators) but express it in terms of real relief (for students).
* **Honest & Gimmick-Free:** We do not use fake tests or misleading metrics. The 30-minute free session must be presented honestly as an eye-pacing assessment.

### Vocabulary Filters
* **AVOID:** Hype-heavy copy ("Read at 10,000 WPM!"), pseudo-scientific jargon that sounds like marketing speak ("visual ingestion calibration"), and passive verbs.
* **USE:** Active, outcome-focused verbs ("lock in focus," "defeat regression," "extract value"), and grounded scientific terms ("desirable difficulty").

## 5. Article Writing Guidelines (Multiple Intelligences Adaptations)
To accommodate different cognitive styles, the bootcamp lessons must be adapted dynamically to the user's primary cognitive archetype. When writing or updating article content, follow these translation blueprints:

### 1. Linguistic (Word Smart)
* **Goal:** Engage through rich narrative prose and elegant language.
* **Layout & Style:** Classical editorial layout using drop-caps, serif typography (`font-serif`), and traditional literary paragraphs.
* **Content:** Compelling stories, metaphors, deep-dive descriptions, and vocabulary-rich vocabulary.

### 2. Logical (Logic Smart)
* **Goal:** Explain concepts as systematic structures and workflows.
* **Layout & Style:** Algorithmic structures using code blocks (`font-mono`), nested logic boxes, and clear input-to-output maps.
* **Content:** Step-by-step algorithms, cause-and-effect calculations, mathematical formulas, and `IF/THEN/ELSE` conditionals.

### 3. Visual (Picture Smart)
* **Goal:** Help the reader "see" the concepts spatially.
* **Layout & Style:** Spatial organization, diagrams, callouts with visual metaphors, and embedded illustration graphics.
* **Content:** Descriptive mind maps, graphic diagrams, visual layout blocks, and color-coded conceptual regions.

### 4. Kinesthetic (Body Smart)
* **Goal:** Connect intellectual concepts to physical actions and muscle memory.
* **Layout & Style:** Procedural manuals, action-oriented checklists, and physically interactive steps.
* **Content:** Step-by-step physical drills, posture corrections, tactile metaphors, and concrete hands-on experiments.

### 5. Musical (Music Smart)
* **Goal:** Frame learning through rhythm, timing, and audio patterns.
* **Layout & Style:** Lyric sheets, rhythmic poems, and soundwave analogies.
* **Content:** Catchy lyrics marked with musical notes (`♪`), rhythm descriptions (e.g., "4/4 Time", "metronome hum"), and auditory mnemonics.

### 6. Interpersonal (People Smart)
* **Goal:** Learn through social exchange, empathy, and collaborative dialogue.
* **Layout & Style:** Chat interface layout, interview transcripts, or debate scripts.
* **Content:** Direct conversations between a mentor and student, Q&A threads, peer-to-peer dialogues, and community-centric applications.

### 7. Intrapersonal (Self Smart)
* **Goal:** Foster self-reflection, personal values, and emotional connection.
* **Layout & Style:** Personal journal entries or quiet meditation prompts.
* **Content:** Introspective writing prompts, deep questioning, connecting concepts to personal identity, goals, and internal motivations.

### 8. Naturalistic (Nature Smart)
* **Goal:** Map abstract theories to natural ecosystems and biological patterns.
* **Layout & Style:** Vintage field guide pages, classifications, and observation boxes.
* **Content:** Ecosystem taxonomies, plant/animal survival analogies, soil/growth models, and weather pattern metaphors.
